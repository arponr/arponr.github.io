Work and Not Work by Arpon Raksit

In translation II

I pretend I am nothing, but then a bird on a wire in the sun, a cloud in the breeze on a mountain… I seem to be something, like these.

In Gurye, as I translate the road signs from speedbump to otter, Princeton’s early spring peeks out from the water, as I once found ochre falling in Cambridge from Gurye.

I sink into here, I drink the things I see or don’t, barley, rice, sugar, peach, pedals translating me between modest elevations, temperatures, degrees of carbonation.

Modesty is measured by wider schemes that I can abandon now.

During translation, is there a point of balance? I imagine perched there I could rest, answering fluently, peacefully, yes, when you say hello, and remaining just so as you leave.
