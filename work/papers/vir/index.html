Work and Not Work by Arpon Raksit

Spectral algebraic theories I: virtualization

Draft available on request

We explain a construction that uniformly produces the derived ∞-category of abelian groups, the ∞-category of derived commutative rings, and the ∞-category of MU-synthetic spectra; we call the construction “virtualization”. These three examples belong to a class of ∞-categories that we call “spectral algebraic theories”, and our results rely on general observations about such ∞-categories and how to construct them. We show that the virtualization construction is characterized by a universal property in the setting of spectral algebraic theories, similar in nature to the theory of animation.
